Loom License and Subscription Model¶

Based on the deployment model chosen, Veritas Multi-Cloud Data Management Platform can be Licensed for metered usage or as per subscription, amount of enterprise data, kind and type of Platform applications deployed and Platform features used.

Licensing Licensing FAQ

Meter: Data Scanned and Analyzed by Loom, per front end TeraByetes, total number of connected enterprise data assets and total number of users

Model: Term Subscription available for 90 days free trial and paid annual term
  1. Isn’t Loom trial free? Customers that want to try out Loom with no SLA could register on the SaaS deployment on Azure. For 90 days it can be tried for a max of # connected data sources and x TB of data with valid Azure credentials. Customers that want to try it on premise will require to purchase the product and deployment consulting services / support fees. Note: Only Default Applications are supported in trial version
  2. What are some of the License sizing guidelines? Customers may purchase a subscription to account for current data scanned and analyzed with Loom as well as their expected growth throughout the term of the subscription. If you are unsure of the growth rate, Veritas recommends planning for 25% growth rate per annum based on Industry average Loom measures the total amount and size of data it manages on a weekly/ monthly / annual basis. In future, we plan to support pay per use model for Cloud based deployments. On premise deployments will be term based with additional costs for supporting various cloud based data sources, depending upon cloud provider fees.
  3. Is Loom offered as a perpetual license? No, it is only offered as a term subscription.
  4. Is there any plan to open source the core functions? May be. Not at this time.
  5. What happens once a customer subscription expires? Customers that cease paying for a Loom subscription will lose entitlement to the product, its services and functions, any product upgrades and maintenance. They can continue to access enterprise data sources as usual but no management or policy- based data control will be allowed, no reports or notifications. None of the direct access to enterprise data will be impacted.
  6. Are customers eligible for future revisions of Loom? Yes, as long they have a valid and active subscription.
Definitions
Term Subscription A licensing model where the customer pays up front for the right to use software for a period of time; with Loom the customers will receive license and Basic or Advanced maintenance entitlement with their product subscription.
